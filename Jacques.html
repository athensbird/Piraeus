Laid-back Bulgarian City

Bulgaria's laid-back capital, Sofia is often overlooked by tourists heading straight to the coast, but they're missing something special. It's no grand metropolis, but it's a largely modern, youthful city, while its old east-meets-west atmosphere is still much evident – a scattering of onion-domed churches, Ottoman mosques and stubborn Red Army monuments shares the skyline with vast malls and glassy five-star hotels.

Pearl of Croatia

Zagreb has culture, arts, music, architecture, gastronomy that make a quality capital city – it's no surprise that the number of visitors has risen sharply in the last couple of years. Croatia's coastal attractions aside, Zagreb has finally been discovered as a popular destination in its own right.

Historical yet Charming

Romania’s capital gets a bad rap, but in fact it's dynamic, energetic and quite fun. It’s where still-unreconstructed communism meets unbridled capitalism; where the soporific forces of the EU meet the passions of the Balkans and Middle East. Many travellers give the city just a night before heading to Transylvania, but we think that’s not enough. Budget at least a few days to take in museums, stroll the parks and hang out at trendy cafes.
